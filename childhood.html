The Foundation: Engineering Since Day One

My formative years laid the groundwork for a lifetime of innovation.

Backyard Olympics

Growing up with my twin brother Eric, we transformed our backyard into a competitive arena. We engineered our own high-jump setups that drew neighborhood crowds, fostering a spirit of innovation and friendly rivalry.

What started as backyard fun became a demonstration of engineering principles. The high jump wasn't just about jumping higher—it was about designing equipment that was safe, adjustable, and exciting for everyone.

This experience taught me that innovation happens when creativity meets practicality, a lesson I apply to every software solution I build.

Twin Bond

Sharing victories and challenges with Eric built unbreakable teamwork.

Community Hub

Our setups became a gathering place for local kids to compete and learn.

Early Engineering

Designing and building equipment sparked my passion for creation.

The DIY Spark

My fascination with electricity led to creating a circling motor—a backyard invention that captivated onlookers. This was the alpha version of my software engineering career, translating physical logic into digital innovation.

What started as curiosity became a demonstration of problem-solving. The motor's success wasn't just about the device; it was about understanding systems and iteration.

This experience taught me that engineering is about turning ideas into reality, a principle I carry into every project today.
